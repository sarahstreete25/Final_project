ART PIECES

'Stop Killing Us'

'Stop Killing Us' is a constructed mixed-media memorial to trans women, papier-mâché faces, arms and hands piled at the base. Around them, the purple beings live their lives on foam and canvas: they head into the world, they discover its inadequacies, they look on with kindness and suggest that there’s a better way.

Price: $350

'What?'

In the piece 'What?' her pupils don’t match, one a glowing blue marble while the other looks out, ornate at the edges. Her full, red lips are pursed but kind; her nose spins out in three directions. Over a cocked right eyebrow, a sort of scrolling tattoo reveals the name “Alexander.” So is she a she after all? Does it matter?

Price: $300

'He/She Ballet Dancer'

He wears a pink tutu, one leg back while the other balances on the floor. Beneath his “skin,” layers of paper painted in oranges, siennas, yellow and greens, webs of words read “I AM GAY/I AM STRAIGHT/I AM/I AM HUMAN.”
